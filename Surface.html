Enceladus , a moon of Saturn, has an icy surface with bright and smooth areas, averaging -200°C(-328°F). It's active geysers,known as "tiger stripes", release water vapor,indicating a subsurface ocean.Hydrothermal vents on the ocean floor likely heat the water,causing eruptions that shape the surface and create features like smooth terrains and ice fracturing. The reflective ice prevents sunlight from penetrating , while organic compounds in the plumes Hong at potential for extraterrestrial life.
